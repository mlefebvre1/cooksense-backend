# Vision

## One-liner

> **CookSense helps the home cook decide what to eat tonight in less than 30
> seconds, and progressively learn how to cook without recipes — clean, fast,
> ad-free.**

If a feature does not serve this sentence, we cut it.

## Why

Modern recipe sites are slow, cluttered with ads and endless backstories.
Skilled home cooks waste time every weeknight just deciding what to make. They
don't need another recipe site — they need a **decision tool**.

## What CookSense is

- A web application (mobile + tablet first) that surfaces curated recipes one
  at a time, Tinder-style.
- A short-list of structured recipes ("concept, key ingredients, essential
  steps") respecting a hard constraint: **30 minutes max in the evening**, with
  optional passive prep in the morning (slow cooker, pressure cooker).
- A "Cooking School" companion that teaches **principles** (ratios, Maillard,
  flavor balance, mother sauces) so users eventually stop needing recipes.

## What CookSense is not

- Not an ad-supported content farm.
- Not a real-time AI recipe generator. Recipes are **pre-generated and curated**.
- Not a social network. Community features (photos, votes) are **post-MVP**.
- Not a meal-planning app — yet (post-MVP).

## North-star metric

**Time-to-decision**: median time between opening the app and opening a recipe
for tonight. Target: **< 30 seconds**.

## Anti-goals

- ❌ Ads, sponsored content, affiliate links.
- ❌ Long-form blog content interleaved with recipes.
- ❌ Auto-generated recipes shown to users without human review.
- ❌ Lock-in: data export must always be possible.
